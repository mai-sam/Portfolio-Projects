Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty was a gift given to us by the French in 1886 and is was to greet immigrants that came to New York as a symbol of freedom. However, if architecture isn't your thing, in order to get to the Statue of Liberty you get on a ferry to reach the 14 acre island known as Liberty Island. The view from the ferry ride of New York is breathtaking. The ferry also drops you off at Ellis Island where millions of immigrants embarked on their American Dream, Ellis Island is now home to the Immigration Muesuem. Technically you can see The Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island for free, however, you have to buy a ferry ticket which is usually 20 dollars. Visiting the Statue of Liberty is an activity that any age can enjoy.

Central Park

Central Park is an urban park located in Manhattan. It is the most visited urban park in the United States, the park is a total of 840 acres which is about two and half miles in length. The park invludes meadows, forests lakes, monuments, and a lot of other stuff. Central Park is a wonderful place for any age to explore as long as they have the stamina to support it. The park is so large that there are multiple other things inside the park itself to check out. Such as The Central Park Zoo that lets you see animals from three global zones- tropical, temperate, and polar. There is also Belvedere Castle which is a castle that is perched on top of a hill and you can see views of the park and the city scape. The entry to the actual park itself is free. But somethings do require tickets to get in such as the Zoo.

Times Square

Times Square is a major commercial intersection, tourist destination, entertainment center, and neighborhood in the Midtown Manhattan section of New York City, at the junction of Broadway and Seventh Avenue. With flashing neon lights and giant digital billboards, Times Square is big and bright. The main junction is filled with popular retailers. You can also walk to the top of the famous red steps to see a complete view of the area. Similar to the top two other items, this is a all ages friendly place to visit and it's free to enter, but make sure to have a few dollars to spend just in case you're hungry or want to do some retail therapy.
