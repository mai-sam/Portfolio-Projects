Come Take a Bite Out of the Big Apple

If you're looking for new delicacies to hunt, consider the fact that just about every neighborhood in New York City has something to offer, by way of their food scenes. The city is one big melting pot and its food scene a catalog of foods new, classic, and globally inspired.

Halal Guys Food Truck

In New York you can see a food truck on every corner of every street, you can close your eyes and walk ten feet and you'll run into one, but the single diamond that stands out among all of them is the Halal Guy's food truck! It can be considered a "New" New York classic. For only $10 dollars you get a combo platter consisting of chicken and beef gyro over rice. If you want something more handheld you can order a pita gyro. Even if it's considered street food, it won't always be so quick. They can have a line that wraps around a block during peak lunch hours. But when you get to the front of the line make sure to ask for extra red and white sauce and they only accept cash, but don't worry, there's always an ATM around the corner.

Ippudo Westside

If you're looking for something classier and want to have a seat, head to Ippudo Westside. They are a Japanese Ramen resturant with their New York location being their first overseas resturant that opened in 2008. With a simple yet classy ambience, they serve Japanese small dishes, sake, but their Tonkotsu Ramen is world renowned and a definite must try! The average meal will run you around $35 to $40 dollars per person not including drink or gratiuity. So they're a good option if you want not break the bank. Don't be shy to slurp the noodles, slurping the noodles in Japanese culture mean you're enjoying your meal!

The Chef's Table at Brooklyn Fare

If you're looking to be really impressed and want to have an experience alongside your meal than make your way to The Chef's Tabe at Brooklyn Fare. They are a three Michelin Star resturant, they focus on a French and Japanese fusion cuisine. They can only fit twenty people in their resturant at once, so make sure to make reservations as soon as you know because those twenty spots are always in demand. The resturant has a complete open kitchen structure, you can see each every chef craft your twenty course meal. The meal will last about two to two and a half hours. However, the meal is gonna cost you a pretty penny. An average meal is gonna cost you around $360 before any drinks. If you want to taste something that you've never tasted and money isn't an issue go to The Chef's Table at Brooklyn Fare
